## Assignment Overview

Write a short story (2,000–3,500 words) that experiments with an unconventional point of view. This draft builds on the voice exercises from Weeks 4–6 and pushes you to try a narrative perspective you haven't used before.

This is a workshop draft — it does not need to be polished. Focus on taking a genuine risk with point of view rather than producing a "finished" story.

## Requirements

- Length: 2,000–3,500 words
- Must use a point of view other than simple past-tense third-person limited
- Include a brief author's note (100–200 words) explaining your POV choice and what you're trying to achieve
- Double-spaced, 12pt font, standard manuscript format
- File format: .docx or .pdf

## Point of View Options to Consider

Second person ("you") — see Lorrie Moore, "How to Become a Writer"
First person plural ("we") — see Julie Otsuka, "The Buddha in the Attic"
Unreliable narrator — see Kazuo Ishiguro, "The Remains of the Day"
Shifting/multiple POV — see Jennifer Egan, "A Visit from the Goon Squad"
Unusual narrator (object, animal, collective) — see George Saunders, "Fox 8"

You are not limited to these — any deliberate, purposeful choice of point of view works.

## Rubric

### Risk & Ambition (30%)
4 — Bold POV choice, fully committed throughout the story
3 — Clear POV choice with some ambitious moments
2 — Safe or conventional POV with minimal experimentation
1 — No evident POV experimentation

### Craft & Technique (30%)
4 — Strong control of language, scene, and pacing
3 — Solid craft with some rough spots
2 — Developing craft; scenes or pacing need work
1 — Significant craft issues throughout

### Author's Note (20%)
4 — Thoughtful reflection that shows awareness of POV effects
3 — Adequate reflection with some insight
2 — Superficial or vague reflection
1 — Missing or unrelated

### Workshop Readiness (20%)
4 — Complete, formatted, submitted on time
3 — Complete but minor formatting issues
2 — Incomplete or late
1 — Not submitted
